# Novelty Verification Summary

This document summarizes the prior-art and novelty checks for the top-ranked solid-state electrolyte candidates. The goal was to identify which candidates have the strongest defensible novelty claims by verifying if exact compositions or close analogues have been reported in academic literature (Google Scholar, arXiv, Nature/npj, ACS, RSC, ScienceDirect).

## Evaluation Criteria
Candidates were assessed based on:
1. **Exact Matches**: Is the specific stoichiometric composition already reported in literature?
2. **Close Analogues**: Are similar compositions (e.g., same elemental components but different stoichiometry) reported?
3. **Novelty Claim**: Does the candidate represent a genuinely new exploration space (e.g., underexplored substitutions, novel high-entropy designs)?

## Key Findings

### Candidates with the Strongest Defensible Novelty Claim

The following candidates demonstrate high novelty (Score 4-5) as no exact matches were found in standard academic searches, indicating they are either completely unexplored or represent highly specific, uncharacterized points within a known phase space:

1. **Li2.8In0.2Sc0.2Yb0.2Lu0.2Zr0.2Cl6 (High-Entropy Halide)**
   - *Novelty*: **Highly Novel (High-Entropy)** (Score: 5)
   - *Rationale*: High configurational entropy (5 M-cation species) design. While the overarching framework is known, this exact multi-cation composition is an underexplored multi-cation design that avoids direct prior art.

2. **LiHfFCl4 (Li-stuffed variant)** & **LiZrFCl4**
   - *Novelty*: **Novel Composition** (Score: 4)
   - *Rationale*: While the soft-cradle non-close-packed structure (LiMXCl4) is very recently reported (Ceder group, 2025), these specific F-substituted and Li-stuffed variants are computational predictions without widespread experimental literature footprint.

3. **Li2Sm2/3Cl4**
   - *Novelty*: **Novel Composition** (Score: 4)
   - *Rationale*: A DFT-predicted halospinel structure. The exact composition lacks an experimental synthesis footprint in broader literature.

4. **Na3GdBr3I3** & **Na3YBr3I3**
   - *Novelty*: **Novel Composition** (Score: 4)
   - *Rationale*: Na halide electrolytes are significantly less explored than Li equivalents. The specific mixed Br/I stoichiometry designed to tune disorder and bottleneck size is a novel computational target.

5. **Li3In0.5Sc0.5Cl6** & **Li3Er0.5Sc0.5Cl6**
   - *Novelty*: **Novel Composition** (Score: 4)
   - *Rationale*: Theoretical mixtures based on ionic potential tuning to force a ccp Cl sublattice. No exact match found, highlighting a gap in the aliovalent substitution series.

### Known/Benchmark Candidates

The search confirmed exact matches or widespread knowledge for several benchmark candidates, which should be excluded from primary novelty claims (Score 1-2):
- **Na2.88Sb0.88W0.12S4**: Champion Na conductor.
- **Li6PS5Cl** / **Li6PS5Br**: Well-known benchmark argyrodite electrolytes.

## Recommendations

For Tier 1A selection, focus should be placed on the **High-Entropy Halides** (e.g., Li2.8In0.2Sc0.2Yb0.2Lu0.2Zr0.2Cl6) and the **computational non-close-packed predictions** (LiHfFCl4, LiZrFCl4) as they offer the most defensible intellectual property and novelty claims. The targeted mixed-halide Na-conductors (Na3GdBr3I3) also represent a highly novel, underexplored niche.
