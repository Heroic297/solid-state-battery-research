# AI-Driven Screening Brief for Novel Solid-State Li/Na Electrolytes

## Executive summary

This screening run produced a ranked set of 44 Li/Na solid-electrolyte candidates and identified one strongest original Tier 1A research hypothesis: **Li3Er0.5Sc0.5Cl6**, a mixed-cation chloride designed to force a cubic-close-packed chloride sublattice while retaining a Li3MCl6-like oxidative window near 4.3 V. The candidate is not presented as experimentally proven or MD-confirmed; it is a compositionally novel, high-priority target generated by descriptor screening and prior-art triage, with the included ASE + MACE/CHGNet/SevenNet workflow ready to validate \( \sigma_{300K} \), \(E_a\), and structure stability.

The broader screen confirms the expected field pattern: sulfide argyrodites and Na thiophosphates/sulfides dominate room-temperature conductivity but have narrow thermodynamic windows, while chloride and oxy/fluorochloride halides offer the best path to >4 V cathode compatibility at the cost of reductive instability against Li metal. Literature evidence supports Li6PS5Cl conductivities around 1-3 mS/cm with a narrow thermodynamic window extended kinetically in practical cells ([Elsevier](https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S0925838819333493)), and halide Li3MCl6 electrolytes are repeatedly identified as high-voltage catholytes with oxidative stability around 4.2-4.3 V but poor direct Li-metal compatibility ([Western Engineering review](https://www.eng.uwo.ca/nanoenergy/publications/2021/pdf/Guofeng-NE-2021.pdf)).

## Method and evidence hierarchy

The pipeline combined five evidence layers: 2020-2026 literature extraction, candidate generation from known solid-electrolyte families, fast-ion structural proxy scoring, stability/interface triage, and prior-art checking. Conductivity values in the final table are labeled by basis: experimental/literature, AIMD/literature prediction, or family/proxy estimate. The local-GPU MLIP-MD pipeline was built and validated in dummy mode, but production 200 ps trajectories were not run in this cloud environment because the user’s local GPU is not directly accessible from the workspace.

The MD workflow implements NVT simulations at 600 K, 800 K, and 1000 K; mean-squared displacement extraction; Arrhenius fitting by \(D(T)=D_0\exp(-E_a/k_BT)\); and Nernst-Einstein conversion by \(\sigma=nq^2D/k_BT\). The dummy validation run passed 19/19 tests and produced internally consistent MSD and Arrhenius plots, but those plots validate the code path rather than any real candidate.

## Mechanistic insights

- **Cubic-close-packed halide lattices are the most robust >4 V design motif.** Li3MCl6-type materials combine large chloride polarizability, high-voltage oxidative stability, and low enough migration barriers when the anion lattice supports 3D Li percolation. The main failure mode is reductive decomposition against Li metal into LiCl plus reduced metals, which makes these materials more natural as catholytes or as part of bilayer electrolytes ([Western Engineering review](https://www.eng.uwo.ca/nanoenergy/publications/2021/pdf/Guofeng-NE-2021.pdf), [Giessen thesis record](https://jlupub.ub.uni-giessen.de/items/fc2123c9-f520-4516-91f0-68a88df33c47)).

- **Mixed M-site disorder is a practical handle for turning 2D halide transport into 3D transport.** The high-entropy chloride result and the Li3Y1-xInxCl6 design logic indicate that cation mixing can induce stacking faults, suppress ordered hcp-like motifs, and promote more isotropic Li diffusion. This motivates the original Li3Er0.5Sc0.5Cl6 and Li3In0.5Er0.5Cl6 hypotheses as specific, testable points in the mixed-lanthanide chloride design space ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10844219/), [UWO Nano Letters PDF](https://www.eng.uwo.ca/nanoenergy/publications/2020/pdf/Xiaona-2020-Nano-letter.pdf)).

- **Non-close-packed LiMXCl4 fluorochlorides may be the highest-upside catholyte class.** Published r2SCAN/AIMD work on LiMXCl4 argues that a soft-cradle mechanism can couple framework motion to Li hops, with LiHfFCl4 and LiZrFCl4 predicted to combine high transport and oxidative limits above 4 V. The trade-off is that these remain synthesis-first and structure-validation-first targets, not mature electrolyte powders ([Ceder group LiMXCl4 paper](https://ceder.berkeley.edu/publications/2025_KyuJung_Grace_LiMXCL4.pdf)).

- **Sulfide and selenide Na conductors are fast but not >4 V electrolytes.** Na3SbS4-family materials can exceed the conductivity target, but their oxidative windows are too low for high-voltage cathodes. This makes Na2.88Sb0.88Mo0.12S4 a useful original conductivity hypothesis but not the best fit for the user’s >4 V success criterion ([Nature Communications](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-019-13178-2), [Ceder group Na interface PDF](https://ceder.berkeley.edu/publications/2017_yaosen_electrode_electrolyte_compat.pdf)).

## Top synthesis and validation briefs

### Li3Er0.5Sc0.5Cl6

Li3Er0.5Sc0.5Cl6 is the strongest original candidate because it is a simple mixed-trivalent chloride with a plausible Li3MCl6 topology, high predicted oxidative stability by family analogy, and no exact prior-art match found in the novelty search. Its central hypothesis is that Er3+/Sc3+ mixing tunes cation-site disorder and stabilizes a ccp chloride sublattice, analogous to known Y/In chloride design rules ([ACS Chemistry of Materials](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.chemmater.1c00555), [UWO Nano Letters PDF](https://www.eng.uwo.ca/nanoenergy/publications/2020/pdf/Xiaona-2020-Nano-letter.pdf)).

- **Synthesis route:** Dry LiCl, ErCl3, and ScCl3 under dynamic vacuum or Ar at 120-180 °C, then ball mill stoichiometric 3:0.5:0.5 chloride precursors in an Ar glovebox using ZrO2 or WC media. Anneal sealed quartz or stainless ampoules at 300-450 °C, with a slow-cool arm and a quench arm to test whether disorder or stacking faults improve transport.
- **Phase validation:** Use air-free powder XRD with Rietveld refinement against C2/m and related Li3MCl6 polymorphs, then verify cation homogeneity by SEM-EDS or STEM-EDS. Use pair distribution function analysis if broadening indicates stacking disorder.
- **Transport validation:** Press cold pellets under dry Ar, sputter Au or use blocking stainless-steel electrodes, and collect EIS from -20 °C to 80 °C to extract \(E_a\). The go/no-go threshold is \( \sigma_{25C} > 10^{-3} \) S/cm and \(E_a < 0.30\) eV.
- **Interface validation:** Run CV and linear-sweep voltammetry against Li-In first, not Li metal, then test LCO composite cathodes with an LPSC anode-side interlayer. Direct Li-metal contact is not expected to be stable for chloride halides ([Giessen thesis record](https://jlupub.ub.uni-giessen.de/items/fc2123c9-f520-4516-91f0-68a88df33c47)).

### Li3In0.5Er0.5Cl6

Li3In0.5Er0.5Cl6 is a lower-risk original chloride candidate because In3+ is known to favor ccp chloride frameworks, while Er3+ adds lanthanide-size disorder that may increase Li-site degeneracy. The candidate is less compositionally exotic than the five-cation high-entropy chloride and therefore easier to synthesize as a mechanistic test of the mixed-cation descriptor ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S221128552401187X), [UWO Nano Letters PDF](https://www.eng.uwo.ca/nanoenergy/publications/2020/pdf/Xiaona-2020-Nano-letter.pdf)).

- **Synthesis route:** Dehydrate LiCl, InCl3, and ErCl3, then mechanochemically mill under Ar. Because InCl3 is volatile and moisture-sensitive, use a sealed milling vial and compare direct milling with solution-assisted acetonitrile or THF routes if phase purity is poor.
- **Phase validation:** Fit XRD against Li3InCl6-like C2/m and Li3ErCl6-like structures, then quantify whether the product is a single mixed phase or a two-phase In/Er chloride mixture. A single ccp-derived phase is required before MD or EIS claims are meaningful.
- **Transport validation:** Measure EIS on dense cold-pressed pellets with variable pressure and temperature. If \( \sigma_{25C} \) is below \(10^{-3}\) S/cm, sweep x in Li3InxEr1-xCl6 around x = 0.4-0.8.
- **Cell validation:** Pair with LCO or NMC cathode composites first, using LPSC or sulfide separator toward the anode. Chloride catholyte operation is the most defensible initial application because direct Li-metal compatibility is poor ([Western Engineering review](https://www.eng.uwo.ca/nanoenergy/publications/2021/pdf/Guofeng-NE-2021.pdf)).

### LiHfFCl4, Li-stuffed variant

LiHfFCl4 is not the cleanest novelty claim because the family has already been computationally proposed, but it is the highest-upside synthesis-first target in the screen. The published LiMXCl4 work predicts a fluorochloride member with oxidative stability around 4.20 V and very high AIMD-derived conductivity for Li-stuffed variants ([Ceder group LiMXCl4 paper](https://ceder.berkeley.edu/publications/2025_KyuJung_Grace_LiMXCL4.pdf)).

- **Synthesis route:** Explore low-temperature halide metathesis from LiF, LiCl, and HfCl4 or HfF4/HfCl4 mixtures in sealed Ta or quartz-compatible ampoules, starting with stoichiometric LiHfFCl4 and Li-excess variants. Because HfCl4 volatility and hydrolysis are major risks, use rigorously dried precursors and sealed-vessel thermal gradients.
- **Phase validation:** Use air-free XRD and Raman/IR to confirm Hf-F/Hf-Cl coordination, then compare against predicted LiMXCl4 chain motifs. If phase purity is difficult, test mechanochemical activation followed by lower-temperature annealing to avoid halide loss.
- **Transport validation:** Run EIS on cold-pressed pellets and compare against MLIP-MD predicted \(E_a\). If conductivity is low, Li stuffing and defect control should be varied before abandoning the structure type.
- **Interface validation:** Treat as catholyte-only initially: LCO at 4.2-4.4 V is the first test, while the anode side should use LPSC or another reductively tolerant interlayer because LiMXCl4 reductive limits are far above Li metal ([Ceder group LiMXCl4 paper](https://ceder.berkeley.edu/publications/2025_KyuJung_Grace_LiMXCL4.pdf)).

## Pre-print-ready outline

### Introduction

The paper should frame the problem as a multi-objective search: room-temperature \( \sigma > 10^{-3} \) S/cm, oxidative stability above 4 V, low electronic conductivity, and manufacturable interfaces. It should position sulfide argyrodites as conductivity benchmarks with interface limitations, halide Li3MCl6 materials as high-voltage catholytes, and LiMXCl4 fluorochlorides as a new non-close-packed design frontier ([Elsevier](https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/S0925838819333493), [Western Engineering review](https://www.eng.uwo.ca/nanoenergy/publications/2021/pdf/Guofeng-NE-2021.pdf), [Ceder group LiMXCl4 paper](https://ceder.berkeley.edu/publications/2025_KyuJung_Grace_LiMXCL4.pdf)).

### Methods

The methods should describe literature mining, candidate family enumeration, thermodynamic and band-gap filtering where data were available, structural-proxy scoring, prior-art search, and the ASE + MLIP-MD validation workflow. The computational claims should be split into three classes: experimentally reported values, published AIMD/DFT values, and new proxy-ranked hypotheses.

### Results

The primary result is the ranked candidate table, with Li3Er0.5Sc0.5Cl6 and Li3In0.5Er0.5Cl6 presented as original testable hypotheses rather than completed discoveries. The secondary result is that LiHfFCl4/LiZrFCl4 and high-entropy chlorides appear to be the best immediate validation targets if the goal is rapid experimental traction.

### Discussion

The discussion should emphasize that the best practical architecture is likely a multilayer electrolyte: a high-voltage chloride or fluorochloride catholyte paired with a sulfide or engineered interlayer at the anode. This follows directly from the mismatch between chloride oxidative strength and chloride reductive instability against Li metal ([Giessen thesis record](https://jlupub.ub.uni-giessen.de/items/fc2123c9-f520-4516-91f0-68a88df33c47)).

### Limitations

No production MLIP-MD, DFT relaxation, BVS calculation, or grand-canonical convex-hull computation was executed for the original proxy candidates in this cloud run. The package therefore constitutes a reproducible discovery hypothesis and validation plan, not a completed first-principles proof.

## Immediate next experiment/computation

The next decisive step is to generate ordered and disordered supercells for Li3Er0.5Sc0.5Cl6 and Li3In0.5Er0.5Cl6, relax them with DFT or a trusted universal MLIP, run the included 600/800/1000 K MD workflow, and only then promote candidates whose extrapolated \( \sigma_{300K} \) exceeds \(10^{-3}\) S/cm with \(E_a < 0.30\) eV.
